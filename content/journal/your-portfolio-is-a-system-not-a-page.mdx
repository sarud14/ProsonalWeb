## The reframe

Most engineer portfolios are a gallery: screenshots, a stack list, a contact form. They show output. But the thing I actually want to communicate is how I think about structure — how I draw boundaries, weigh trade-offs, and keep a system legible as it grows. A gallery cannot carry that.

## What it changed

Once I treated the site as a system, the decisions got easier. Every page became a module with a clear job. Metadata moved to monospace. Case studies got a fixed six-part spine so they are comparable. The constraint did the design work for me.
